2025 Goals

“There are times when nuances are less important than sincerity” - Raphael To (Sorry I’m quoting you Raphael, this will only become a habit if you keep being inspiring).

To be honest, right now I’m in a mood where I can’t wait for January to end. That doesn’t mean there aren’t good things that happened and positive takeaways from this month (there’s always positive takeaways!) but that’s how I feel. However, I am committed to updating this blog once a month so I’ll talk about the good things that happened, and then focus on my goals for the upcoming year! (The year that I decided starts in February and January never happened).

I have some specific goals and some more vague ones. Maybe I’ll even set a few more while writing this, but I’ll start with my two biggest goals.

My number one goal is to pass the JLPT N2. For those who don’t know what the JLPT is, it is a series of standardized Japanese tests that are recognized across the country as certifications of language ability. It goes from levels N5-N1, N5 being the lowest level and N1 being the highest.

It tests a mix of vocabulary, kanji (Japanese sino-characters), reading and listening. Two years ago in December I took and passed the JLPT N3. N3 is considered intermediate Japanese, while the jump from N3 to N2 is considered advanced Japanese and N1 is considered Japanese mastery. However, since the test doesn’t include speaking or written portions it is quite questionable how truly well it measures the “mastery” of the Japanese language.

That being said, it is still a certification and a concrete goal I can work for and study for. Because of that, I find the test to be highly motivating for my own personal studies of the language.

Another goal I have is to run my first marathon this year. I’ve never run one before, and my first half marathon was in the fall of 2023. I haven’t run a half since then, but since last year I dealt with graduating and moving to Japan to start my first job, I didn’t have a lot of time or energy to focus on training. I really have two goals regarding this I suppose, because I would also like to try running a half marathon this year as well.

The half marathon I plan on running is the 17 END Half Marathon event hosted in Miyakojima! It’s a local running event held in November. It’s not really on Miyakojima, but on Irabujima and Shimojishima, two islands connected to Miyakojima by bridge and governed by the Miyakojima municipality.

The Marathon event I wish to run in is the Naha Marathon. Naha is the largest city in Okinawa, and the event is hosted in early December. Upon writing this, I realized that the Marathon last year fell on the exact same day as the winter JLPT test examination date. While I will take the JLPT in the summer, if I don’t pass I may want to take it again in the winter. If that happens, I will probably shoot for the Ishigaki Marathon.

This is a map of the Ryukyu Archipelago for some reference. The most notable islands are Amami, Okinawa, Miyako and Yaeyama which is much less inhabited than and falls under the municipality of Ishigaki which is just to the right of it.

The Ishigaki Marathon happens in January, so I will have to run it in 2026. However, I’m highly interested in the event anyway as it will give me a good opportunity to visit Ishigaki. Ishigaki is an island and a municipality that (similar to Miyakojima) governs a cluster of islands that makes Japan’s Western most point. It is also home to the largest mountain in all of Okinawa, Mt. Otomo, and has its own tropical beaches and deep forests to be explored.

Those are my two biggest goals. Some other smaller goals involve traveling to a new foreign country (currently plans to visit the Philippines or Vietnam are in the works), updating this blog at least once a month, getting better at taking pictures (more on that later), making more friends, developing more resources for outer Island JETs in Okinawa to connect to a wider community, getting better at Sanshin, start studying Chinese (probably somewhat casually) and climbing Mt. Fuji. I know it’s a lot, but I’m excited to see what I can achieve this year!

Ok, now that I’ve mentioned my goals I want to write a little bit about how this month has been for me.

January is the coldest month in Miyakojima. The weather ranges from low 50s to high 70s. That sounds not that bad (and it really isn’t) except that nobody has or uses heaters (including me). As I’m writing this it is 63 degrees outside and all the windows in the school are open. It really isn’t that cold, but it feels cold when there’s no escape from the weather.

So, about that goal I mentioned where I want to get better at taking pictures. I bought my first camera this month! It’s a Sony Alpha 6100, for anyone curious, and it came with a wide angle lens and a zoom lens.
